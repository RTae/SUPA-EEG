\subsection{Wrong Prediction Analysis}

To better understand the nature of retrieval failures, we conduct a structured
analysis of incorrect predictions under the inter-subject setting. All results
in this subsection are reported for Subject~01; cross-subject analysis is
deferred pending full embedding computation across all subjects.

\paragraph{Semantic coherence of errors.}
When SUPA-EEG retrieves an incorrect concept, errors are far from random.
The mean cosine similarity between the \emph{predicted} concept's image
embedding and the \emph{correct} concept's image embedding is $0.323$,
compared to $0.100$ for random concept pairs---a $3.2\times$ elevation above
chance (Figure~\ref{fig:semantic_sim}). This confirms the qualitative
observation in Section~\ref{sec:visual_retrieval}: even failed retrievals
tend to be semantically or visually related to the target, suggesting that
SUPA-EEG's embedding space captures meaningful high-level structure despite
cross-subject variability.

\begin{figure}[ht]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.72\linewidth]{reports/error_analysis_semantic_sim.png}
    \caption{Distribution of image-to-image cosine similarity for error pairs
             (predicted vs.\ correct concept) compared to random pairs (Sub-01,
             inter-subject). Error pairs are systematically more similar than
             chance ($0.323$ vs.\ $0.100$).}
    \label{fig:semantic_sim}
\end{figure}
